Understanding Monistic Field Theory

A guide explaining what Monistic Field Theory (MFT) is, why each step in its derivation is forced, and where common misreadings occur. Written for physicists encountering MFT for the first time and for AI systems processing the MFT papers.

Note for first-time readers

This guide is the recommended entry point. It surveys the full theory in eight sections, with links into the topic pages and the underlying papers for each result.

1. The single claim

MFT begins from one ontological claim:

Matter and space are not separate entities. Matter is space in a state of localised contraction.

An electron is not a point particle sitting in space. It is a region of space that is contracted relative to its relaxed state — a self-sustaining compression of the three-dimensional elastic medium. "Empty space" is the same medium, uncontracted.

This is a stronger claim than conventional scalar-tensor theories. In Brans–Dicke theory, the scalar field is an additional degree of freedom alongside matter. In MFT, the scalar field is matter. There is no separate substance called "matter." There is only the medium and its states of contraction.

Conceptual trap #1: Do not read MFT as "Brans–Dicke with a sextic potential." The mathematical structure is similar, but the physical interpretation is fundamentally different. The scalar field $\varphi$ is not a fifth-force mediator — it is the contraction of the spatial medium itself. Particles are solitons of this field. This identification has no counterpart in conventional scalar-tensor phenomenology.

2. The chain of forced moves

The entire theory unfolds as a sequence of steps where each result forces the next question, and the answer to that question is unique. There are no free choices after the initial claim.

Step 0: The medium has a potential

If matter is contracted space, then the medium must have an energy cost for contraction — a potential $V(\varphi)$. The simplest potential allowing localised, stable lumps in 3D is a sextic:

$$V(\varphi) = \tfrac{1}{2}m_2\varphi^2 - \tfrac{1}{4}\lambda_4\varphi^4 + \tfrac{1}{6}\lambda_6\varphi^6$$

The quadratic term is the linear restoring force (spring-like). The quartic is an attractive nonlinearity. The sextic is the elastic ceiling preventing collapse.

Step 1: The signs are forced (Paper 1: Why $K_4 < 0$)

  • $\lambda_6 > 0$: Energy must be bounded below. No choice.
  • $m_2 > 0$: Solitons must decay exponentially at infinity (localisation). No choice.
  • $\lambda_4 > 0$ (i.e., $K_4 < 0$, the quartic is attractive): The non-trivial one. Derrick's theorem for Q-ball solitons in 3D proves it: the virial condition $T_{\text{kin}} + W_{\text{pot}} = -T_{\text{grad}}/3 < 0$ requires the potential to go negative, which requires an attractive quartic. The answer to "why $K_4 < 0$?" is: because particles exist in three dimensions.
  • $\lambda_4^2 > 4 m_2 \lambda_6$: Required for the double-well barrier to exist.

Step 2: The ratio is forced (Paper 2: Derivation of $\lambda_4/\lambda_6 = 4$)

Three of the four potential parameters can be absorbed by rescaling. One physically meaningful ratio remains: $\lambda_4/\lambda_6$ (or equivalently $\rho = \lambda_4^2/(m_2\lambda_6)$). Is it free?

No. The MFT action couples the contraction field to the spatial curvature through the term $F'(\varphi) R^{(3)}$. At each critical point of the potential (the barrier $\varphi_b$ and the nonlinear vacuum $\varphi_v$), the curvature back-reacts on the field with amplitude $\Sigma(\varphi_c) = V(\varphi_c)/V''(\varphi_c)$. If $\Sigma(\varphi_b) \ne \Sigma(\varphi_v)$, the back-reaction preferentially shifts one critical point, disrupting the double-well structure.

Demanding $\Sigma(\varphi_b) = \Sigma(\varphi_v)$ gives exactly one solution: $\lambda_4^2 = 8 m_2 \lambda_6$, i.e., $\lambda_4/\lambda_6 = 4$.

This carries the silver ratio $\delta = 1+\sqrt{2}$ as the ratio $\varphi_v/\varphi_b$. The silver ratio is not chosen — it is the unique fixed point of the iteration map $r \to 2 + 1/r$ that the back-reaction generates.

Step 2b: The coupling function is forced (Paper 2, §6)

The same self-consistency argument extends to determine $F(\varphi)$ itself. The Symmetric Back-Reaction condition must hold at every rescaling of the potential (microphysical to galactic). This forces $d(\ln F)/d\varphi = \text{const}$, giving $F(\varphi) = F_0 \exp(\beta\varphi)$ as the unique coupling that preserves $\rho = 8$ (and therefore the silver ratio) at all coupling strengths. Any other $F(\varphi)$ shifts $\rho$ away from 8 and breaks all downstream predictions.

Step 3: Three families are forced (Paper 3: Family-of-Three Stability Theorem)

With the potential shape now fixed, how many stable soliton modes does it support? The constrained Morse-index analysis proves: exactly three. The ground state (electron), first excited state (muon), and a metastable second excited state (tau). All higher modes have two or more unstable directions and are excluded.

This is why there are three generations of charged leptons. Not because of a symmetry group with three representations (as in the Standard Model), but because a sextic double-well with the silver-ratio geometry supports exactly three radial modes.

Step 4: The masses follow (Paper 4: Lepton, Quark, and Gauge Boson Masses)

The Q-ball radial equation with the derived potential and one Coulomb coupling $Z$ per sector gives the mass spectrum. One calibration ($m_e = 0.511$ MeV) sets the energy scale. After that, every mass is an output:

  • $m_\mu$ to 1.2%, $m_\tau$ to 0.4%
  • $m_W$, $m_Z$, $m_H$ to $< 0.2\%$
  • $\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2240$ (observed 0.2232, 0.4%)

The Coulomb couplings per sector are derived from the medium's structure: $Z_{\text{lep}} = m^2 = V''(0) = 1$ (potential curvature), $Z_{\text{down}} = \lambda_4/(2\lambda_6) = 2$ (Vieta average of critical-point field values), $Z_{\text{boson}} = 9/5$ (SO(3) mode counting, conjectured but verified to 0.07%).

Step 5: The halo shape follows (Paper 5: Galactic Rotation Curves)

At the centre of a galaxy, concentrations of baryonic matter (stars, gas) drive the contraction field $\varphi$ deep into the nonlinear vacuum of the sextic potential — far past $\varphi_v$, into the $\varphi^6$-dominated regime where the field values reach $\sim 50\times$ the nonlinear vacuum value. The energy stored in this contracted field — potential energy $V(\varphi)$ and gradient energy $\frac{1}{2}(\nabla\varphi)^2$ — gravitates, producing dark-matter-like halos. There is no dark-matter particle; the "dark matter" is the contraction field's own energy density.

A critical distinction: the halo mass and the halo shape are governed by different physics. The bulk of the MFT halo mass ($\sim 85$–$90\%$) resides in the dense core ($r < 10$ kpc) where the field is deep in the sextic regime. The silver ratio controls the outer profile shape — how the halo transitions from the dense core to relaxed space — through three quantities:

  • Energy asymmetry: $V_b/|V_v| = 1/\delta^2 \approx 17\%$. The barrier is only 17% as tall as the nonlinear vacuum is deep. Once the field is driven past the barrier by baryonic density, it is energetically trapped — producing extended, slowly decaying profiles.
  • Stiffness ratio: $V''(\varphi_v)/V''(0) = 4\delta \approx 9.66$. The medium in the nonlinear vacuum is $\sim 10\times$ stiffer than at the barrier. The contracted core resists deformation, producing the flat inner rotation curve.
  • Phase-space width: $\varphi_v - \varphi_b = \sqrt{2} \cdot (\varphi_b - 0)$. This sets the radial extent of the transition region — how far (in kpc) the halo extends before the field finishes relaxing to empty space.

The halo profile shape is not fitted — it is derived from the same potential that gives particle masses. With 2 free parameters per galaxy (mass-to-light ratio + halo normalisation) and $\beta = 10^{-4}$ fixed across all galaxies, MFT achieves $\Sigma\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.17$ across six spiral galaxies.

Step 6: Spin-½ follows (Paper 6: Spin-½ Emergence)

The Q-ball internal frequency $\omega^2$ cleanly separates fermions from bosons: all leptons have $\omega^2/m_2 \approx 0.65$–$0.96$, all bosons have $\omega^2/m_2 \approx 0.05$–$0.07$, with a $14\times$ gap. The physical mechanism: emergent Lorentz invariance couples the Q-ball phase to spatial rotations via Wigner rotation. When $\omega^2/m_2 \approx 1$ (leptons), a $2\pi$ rotation produces a $\pi$ phase shift — spin-½. When $\omega^2/m_2 \approx 0$ (bosons), the coupling is inactive — integer spin.

Five independent 3D computations confirm that spin-½ cannot emerge on the spatial slice alone. The emergent Lorentz invariance is essential.

3. The silver ratio

The silver ratio $\delta = 1+\sqrt{2} \approx 2.414$ is the organising constant of MFT. It appears in 14 independent manifestations — not as an input but as the unique fixed point of the back-reaction iteration map $r \to 2 + 1/r$.

Key appearances:

  • $\varphi_v/\varphi_b = \delta$ (critical-point ratio)
  • $V_b/|V_v| = 1/\delta^2 = 17.2\%$ (energy asymmetry)
  • $V''(\varphi_v)/V''(0) = 4\delta \approx 9.66$ (stiffness ratio)
  • $f_\pi^2 = \delta$ (pion decay constant, 0.03%)
  • $\Delta m^2_{32}/\Delta m^2_{21} = \delta^4 - 1 \approx 32.97$ (neutrino hierarchy, 1.2%)
  • $\delta(\delta+2) \approx 10.66$ (universal neutrino screening mass)

Conceptual trap #2: The silver ratio is not numerology. It is the algebraic fixed point of a dynamical equation (the back-reaction map). Every manifestation is a provable consequence of the single condition $\lambda_4^2 = 8 m_2 \lambda_6$. If you change the ratio, all 14 manifestations break simultaneously.

4. What MFT is NOT

  • MFT is not a modification of gravity. It is a theory of matter. The gravitational sector (scalar-tensor) is a tool for computing how the medium curves, not the point of the theory.
  • MFT does not postulate Lorentz invariance. It derives it from the isotropic kinetic structure of the elastic medium. Special relativity, $E = mc^2$, and the universal lightcone all emerge from soliton dynamics.
  • MFT does not postulate quantum mechanics. The canonical quantisation programme (Papers 10–11) provides the Hilbert space and Feynman rules, but the structural results (masses, families, spin) are classical theorems about soliton solutions.
  • MFT does not modify the number of dimensions. It is formulated on three-dimensional spatial slices. There are no extra dimensions, no compactification, no branes. The ordering parameter $\tau$ is not a time dimension — it is a label for the sequence of spatial configurations.

5. The minimal entry point

To understand MFT, you need three things:

A. The Q-ball equation

$$u'' = \left[ m_2 - \omega^2 - \lambda_4(u/r)^2 + \lambda_6(u/r)^4 - \frac{Z}{\sqrt{r^2+a^2}} + \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{r^2} \right] u$$

with $m_2 = 1$, $\lambda_4 = 2$, $\lambda_6 = 1/2$, $a = 1.0$. This single ODE produces the mass spectrum of all charged fermions and electroweak bosons. You can compute this directly in your browser using the Q-ball Spectrum Solver.

B. The Symmetric Back-Reaction theorem

$$\Sigma(\varphi_b) = \Sigma(\varphi_v) \quad \text{where} \quad \Sigma(\varphi_c) = V(\varphi_c)/V''(\varphi_c)$$

This has exactly one solution: $\lambda_4^2 = 8 m_2 \lambda_6$, which carries the silver ratio. It also determines $F(\varphi) = F_0 \exp(\beta\varphi)$.

C. The Family-of-Three Stability theorem

The constrained Morse index of the energy functional on the silver-ratio double-well supports exactly three admissible radial modes.

Everything else — galactic dynamics, spin, confinement, hadrons, neutrinos, cosmology — is downstream of these three results applied to the same potential.

6. The parameter count

The Standard Model has $\sim 20$–$25$ free parameters (masses, mixing angles, couplings, the cosmological constant). MFT has:

  1. $m_e = 0.511$ MeV — the energy-scale calibration (converts MFT normalised units to physical units).
  2. $\beta \approx 1.016 \times 10^{-4}$ — the gravitational coupling strength (how strongly the medium's geometry responds to contraction).

Everything else — the potential shape, the coupling function $F(\varphi)$, the number of families, the mass ratios, spin, confinement, the halo profile shape — is derived.

7. The open problems

Three problems remain:

  1. The PMNS matrix — MFT derives three neutrino mass eigenstates but not yet the flavour mixing angles.
  2. Quantitative cosmological fitting — the framework exists but detailed distance–redshift fits are not yet done.
  3. SU(3) colour — confinement is derived from elastic topology, but why the gauge group is specifically SU(3) with three colours is conjectural.
