About this calculation

The hierarchy ratio (closed-form)

The MFT sextic potential V(φ) = m²φ²/2 - λ₄φ⁴/4 + λ₆φ⁶/6 has three critical points: φ = 0 (linear vacuum), φ_b (barrier), φ_v (nonlinear vacuum). The Family-of-Three Theorem (P3) associates one neutrino with each.

The mass-squared splitting at each critical point is proportional to V²(φ_i) — the square of the potential energy — arising from second-order gravitational back-reaction through the F(φ)R coupling. The hierarchy ratio is therefore:

Δm²₃₂ / Δm²₂₁ = [V²(φ_v) - V²(φ_b)] / [V²(φ_b) - V²(0)]

When the silver-ratio condition λ₄² = 8 m² λ₆ is satisfied, the potential values at the critical points are exactly:

V(0) = 0,  V(φ_b) = 1/(3δ),  V(φ_v) = -δ/3

Substituting and simplifying gives:

Δm²₃₂ / Δm²₂₁ = δ⁴ - 1 = (1+√2)⁴ - 1 = 16 + 12√2 ≈ 32.97

This is parameter-free. It depends only on the silver ratio δ = 1+√2, which is itself the unique fixed point of the gravitational back-reaction map. Compare to the observed PDG value 32.58 ± 0.3 — agreement at 1.2%.

The absolute masses (one-loop)

The absolute neutrino masses follow from the one-loop gravitational self-energy in the Einstein frame, with the conformal mass correction δm²(φ_i) = 6 β² V(φ_i) and a universal screening mass M²_s = V''(φ_v) + V''(0) (the same for all three neutrinos because each propagates through the same bulk medium).

The 3D one-loop integral I = ∫ d³k/(2π)³ × 1/(k² + M²)³ = 1/(32π M³). Combining:

m_νᵢ = 3 β² |V(φᵢ)| × √I × (m_e/E_e)

At silver-ratio condition with β = 1.016 × 10⁻⁴: m_ν₂ ≈ 8.7 meV, m_ν₃ ≈ 50.4 meV. Compare these with the PDG bounds (Σm_ν < 120 meV) and the observed splittings Δm²₂₁ ≈ 7.53 × 10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₃₂ ≈ 2.453 × 10⁻³ eV².

Why the silver-ratio condition is forced

The condition λ₄² = 8 m² λ₆ is not chosen — it is forced by the Symmetric Back-Reaction Theorem (P2 in the corpus): self-consistency of the contraction field's gravitational back-reaction at both vacua. This is the same condition that produces the lepton mass hierarchy (P4), the pion decay constant (f_π² = δ at 0.03%, P8), the spin-½ classification (14× ω² gap, P6), and eleven other measurable consequences.

The silver ratio is the deepest structural commitment of MFT. Watch the sensitivity plot above as you vary ρ: the hierarchy ratio varies steeply near ρ = 8. A 10% deviation in λ₄² doubles the error against observed Δm²₃₂/Δm²₂₁ from 1.2% to ~64%. This is what makes the prediction falsifiable: a precision improvement in the observed ratio could either lock down the silver-ratio condition further or rule it out.
