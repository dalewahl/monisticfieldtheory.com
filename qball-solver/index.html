About this calculation

The generalised ℓ-dependent Q-ball equation, u″ = [m₂ − ω² − λ₄(u/r)² + λ₆(u/r)⁴ − Z/√(r²+a²) + ℓ(ℓ+1)/r²] · u, admits a discrete spectrum of soliton solutions for any given potential. Each solution is characterised by its node count, angular momentum ℓ, central amplitude φ_core, internal frequency ω², and energy E = ω²·Q (where Q = ∫u² dr is the Noether charge). ℓ = 0 produces scalar solitons (leptons, quarks, Higgs); ℓ = 1 produces vector solitons (W, Z, photon).

ω² is not a free parameter — it is an eigenvalue of the equation, fixed by the requirement that u(r) → 0 as r → ∞ for each discrete solution. The solver scans ω² ∈ [0.05, 0.99] and finds the soliton solutions automatically.

The Standard Model particle families are identified with specific entries in this spectrum, by sector:

  • Charged leptons (Z=1, ℓ=0): the (e, μ, τ) triple. Z=1 is derived from the potential curvature m² = V″(0) = 1.
  • Up-type quarks (Z=1, ℓ=0): the (u, c, t) triple. Same Z as leptons; same field-space regimes.
  • Down-type quarks (Z=2, ℓ=0): the (d, s, b) triple. Z=2 is derived as λ₄/(2λ₆), the Vieta average of the potential's critical-point field values.
  • Gauge bosons (Z=9/5, ℓ=1 for W,Z; ℓ=0 for Higgs): the (W, Z, H) triple. Z=9/5 is conjectured from SO(3) mode counting. The Weinberg angle emerges as sin²θ_W = 1 − (E_W/E_Z)².
  • Photon: ℓ=1, Z=0 — masslessness derived from the Q-ball equation (no Coulomb well → no bound state).

The Family-of-Three Stability Theorem (P3 of the corpus) shows that the three-family structure is a structural consequence of constrained Morse-index analysis: only the n=0 and n=1 modes are stable, n=2 is metastable, and n≥3 modes are multiply unstable.

Free parameter count. The MFT framework operates with two ingredients per sector: the universal potential ratio λ₄/λ₆ = 4 (derived from the Symmetric Back-Reaction Theorem), and the Coulomb coupling Z (derived per sector except Z_boson which is conjectured). One overall energy scale per sector is set by anchoring to one observed mass (m_e for leptons, m_t for up-quarks, m_b for down-quarks, m_W for bosons). All other masses in each sector — plus the Weinberg angle — emerge from the Q-ball equation.
