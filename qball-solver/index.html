About this calculation

The Q-ball equation u″ = [m₂ − ω² − λ₄(u/r)² + λ₆(u/r)⁴ − Z/√(r²+a²)] · u admits a discrete spectrum of soliton solutions for any given potential. Each solution is characterised by its node count, central amplitude φ_core, internal frequency ω², and energy E = ω²·Q (where Q = ∫u² dr is the Noether charge).

ω² is not a free parameter — it is an eigenvalue of the equation, fixed by the requirement that u(r) → 0 as r → ∞ for each discrete solution. The solver scans ω² ∈ [0.05, 0.99] and finds the soliton solutions automatically.

The charged lepton sector of the Standard Model is identified with three specific entries in this spectrum: the n=0 ground state in linear vacuum (electron, φ_core ≈ 0.02), an n=0 mode at the barrier (muon, φ_core ≈ 0.71), and an n=1 metastable mode in the nonlinear vacuum (tau, φ_core ≈ 1.93). The Family-of-Three Stability Theorem (P3 of the corpus) shows that this count is a structural consequence of constrained Morse-index analysis.

Free parameter count. The lepton sector operates with effectively one free parameter: m_e = 0.511 MeV calibrates the energy scale. The potential parameters m₂, λ₄, λ₆ are linked by the silver-ratio condition λ₄² = 8 m₂ λ₆. Z = 1 and a = 1 are fixed by physics within MFT. m₂ = 1 is a unit choice. All three lepton masses, plus the predictions for the up-type quark sector, follow.
