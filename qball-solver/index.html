About this solver

This solver finds soliton solutions of the Q-ball equation in MFT by shooting from r = 0 and matching the boundary condition u(∞) = 0. For a given internal frequency ω², the equation may admit zero, one, or several normalisable soliton solutions, distinguished by their node count and central amplitude φ_core = u′(0).

The potential V(φ) = m₂/2·φ² − λ₄/4·φ⁴ + λ₆/6·φ⁶ has a barrier at φ_b = √((λ₄ − √(λ₄² − 4m₂λ₆))/(2λ₆)) and a second minimum at φ_v = √((λ₄ + √(λ₄² − 4m₂λ₆))/(2λ₆)). When the silver-ratio condition λ₄² = 8 m₂ λ₆ holds, these take the values φ_b² = 1/√2 and φ_v² = √2 = δ − 1, with δ = 1 + √2 the silver ratio.

Solitons whose core amplitude lies in the linear region (φ < φ_b/2) correspond to the lighter family members (electron, up quark); those near the barrier (0.5 φ_b < φ < 1.1 φ_b) correspond to the second family (muon, charm); and those in the nonlinear vacuum (φ > 1.1 φ_b) correspond to the third family (tau, top).

Mass calibration: with m₂ = 1, λ₄ = 2, λ₆ = 0.5, Z = 1, the electron's soliton energy E ≈ 0.00427 in MFT units corresponds to 0.511 MeV, giving the conversion factor MFT_TO_MEV ≈ 119.67.
