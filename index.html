Monistic Field Theory

A unified field theory treating space and matter as a single elastic medium governed by a contraction field φ with sextic potential. Particles are Q-ball solitons of φ. The Standard Model mass spectrum, the Weinberg angle, and galactic rotation curves emerge from two parameters.

What is MFT?

Monistic Field Theory models space as a three-dimensional elastic medium described by a scalar contraction field φ(x). The field's dynamics follow from a sextic potential:

V(φ) = ½ m₂φ² − ¼ λ₄φ⁴ + ⅙ λ₆φ⁶, with the constraint λ₄² = 8m₂λ₆.

The constraint produces the silver ratio δ = 1+√2 as a structural fixed point of the back-reaction iteration r → 2 + 1/r. δ governs the potential's geometry: its critical points, stiffness ratios, and the discrete spectrum of soliton solutions.

Fundamental particles are Q-ball solitons of φ — localised, time-oscillating configurations carrying conserved charge. The same field equation, with sector-specific Coulomb coupling Z, produces the charged leptons (Z=1), up-type quarks (Z=1), down-type quarks (Z=2), and electroweak gauge bosons (Z=9/5). Galactic rotation curves emerge from the same potential mapped to gravitational scales.

The framework operates with two calibration constants: m_e = 0.511 MeV (energy scale) and β ≈ 10⁻⁴ (gravitational coupling). The Standard Model requires approximately 19 independent mass parameters; MFT reproduces them from these two.

Structural results

Exactly three families
Constrained Morse-index analysis: mphys(un) = max(0, n−1). Two stable, one metastable, all higher modes excluded. Falsifiable: a fourth charged lepton refutes the theorem.
Top non-hadronisation
Top quark sits at the n=2 metastable mode in the up-type sector — quark-sector analogue of the tau lepton. Its short lifetime is structural metastability, not coincidence.
Spin-½ classification
Q-ball internal frequency η = ω²/m₂ separates fermions (η ≈ 0.93–0.96) from bosons (η ≈ 0.05–0.07) cleanly across all six fundamental species. 14× gap, no new fields required.
14 silver-ratio manifestations
δ = 1+√2 appears across powers δ⁻², δ⁻¹, δ, δ², δ⁴, plus δ(δ+2). Each manifestation is provably a consequence of the single algebraic constraint λ₄² = 8m₂λ₆.
Confinement without colour
Virial theorem: nonzero topological charge configurations cannot be separated to infinity at finite energy. Confinement is structural elasticity.
Non-singular black holes
The contraction field saturates at φ_v before reaching infinite curvature. Black-hole interiors have finite density; no physical singularity.
Renormalisability resolved
Q-ball ansatz separates variables exactly. Reduces to a 3D problem where [λ₆]=0 is marginal and V1-loop is closed-form finite.
Dark matter as field relaxation
Galactic halos emerge as elastic-medium relaxation around baryonic cores. No new substance; the 17.2% energy asymmetry (=1/δ²) controls relaxation.
σ-meson at mσ = 2fπ
Predicted 372 MeV, within the PDG f₀(500) pole range. Direct identity at the silver-ratio vacuum, parameter-free.
Two parameters total
m_e = 0.511 MeV and β ≈ 10⁻⁴. All particle masses, family count, Weinberg angle, neutrino hierarchies, hadronic spectrum, and galactic dynamics emerge from these plus the silver-ratio condition.

Open problems

Three substantive unresolved questions remain in the framework: the PMNS mixing matrix (eigenstates derived but not the rotation), quantitative cosmology beyond schematic late-time acceleration, and the structural derivation of SU(3) selection.

Negative results — including five ruled-out spin-½ mechanisms and four ruled-out neutrino approaches — are documented in the corpus.

Corpus structure

The papers fall into four groups:

  • Foundations (P0–P2) — Field equations, potential structure, k₄ derivation, λ ratio derivation.
  • Particle sector (P3–P5) — Family-of-Three Stability Theorem, lepton/quark/boson masses, microphysics.
  • Astrophysical sector (P6–P10) — Galactic rotation curves, cosmology, compact objects, gravitational tests.
  • Quantum and field-theoretic structure (P11–P15) — Quantum completion, propagators, scattering, 3D finiteness, spin-½ emergence.
