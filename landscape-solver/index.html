About this visualization

What ρ controls

The MFT sextic potential V6(φ) = m²φ²/2 − λ4φ⁴/4 + λ6φ⁶/6 has three free coefficients. Two of them set the units of φ and V, leaving one dimensionless shape parameter:

ρ = λ4² / (m² λ6)

Everything physically meaningful about the potential's shape depends only on ρ. Solving V'(φ) = 0 gives critical points φ² = (λ4 ± √(λ4² − 4m²λ6)) / (2λ6), which depend on the discriminant and ratios — i.e., on ρ alone.

The five regimes

  • ρ < 4: discriminant negative. No critical points other than φ = 0. Single well, no barrier, no nonlinear vacuum. Soliton solutions don't exist. Incompatible with MFT.
  • ρ = 4: discriminant zero. Inflection point at φ = √(λ4/2λ6). The double-well is just coming into existence — boundary case.
  • 4 < ρ < 8: barrier exists but the double-well is asymmetric. The back-reaction strengths differ at φ_b and φ_v. Symmetric Back-Reaction is violated.
  • ρ = 8 (silver-ratio condition): Σ(φ_b) = Σ(φ_v) holds exactly. The field-space ratio φ_v/φ_b equals the silver ratio δ = 1+√2. V(φ_b) = +1/(3δ), V(φ_v) = −δ/3. The hierarchy ratio ν₃²/ν₂² = δ⁴ − 1.
  • ρ > 8: the double-well becomes increasingly skewed in the opposite direction — barrier shrinks, nonlinear vacuum deepens. The hierarchy ratio overshoots δ⁴ − 1.

The silver-ratio condition's algebraic origin

At ρ = 8: V(φ_b) = +1/(3δ), V(φ_v) = −δ/3. These are exact algebraic consequences of substituting φb = √(2−√2), φv = √(2+√2) into V6. The fact that they appear with the same factor of 1/3 and reciprocal coefficients of δ is what produces the silver ratio's appearance in the hierarchy ratio:

Δm²₃₂/Δm²₂₁ = [V²(φ_v) − V²(φ_b)] / [V²(φ_b) − V²(0)]
= [δ²/9 − 1/(9δ²)] / [1/(9δ²)]
= δ⁴ − 1 = 16 + 12√2 ≈ 32.97

What this visualizer is, and isn't

This is a visualization of the bulk potential V(φ), computed in closed form as you move the slider. It's not a soliton energy landscape (which would be E(φ_core) at fixed Q — see energy_landscape_doublewell.py for that calculation, which takes ~3 minutes on a workstation and would be impractical in the browser). The F3 visualizer shows the constrained Morse-index analysis at fixed parameters; this visualizer shows the parameter space itself.
