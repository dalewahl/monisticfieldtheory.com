About this calculation

The MFT galactic equation

On galactic scales the contraction field δ(r) satisfies a nonlinear Poisson-like equation with the same silver-ratio sextic structure (rescaled to galactic units):

∇²δ + (2/r) dδ/dr = (1/κ)[m²_g δ + λ_4_g δ³ + λ_6_g δ⁵ + β ρ_baryon(r)]

The gravitational sextic coefficients (m²_g, λ_4_g, λ_6_g) are derived from the master sextic potential by the Master-to-Gravitational mapping in P5; the silver-ratio condition λ_4²_g = 8 m²_g λ_6_g propagates exactly. The boundary conditions are dδ/dr(R_min) = 0 (regularity at the centre) and δ(R_max) = 0 (vacuum at infinity).

The MFT halo

Once δ(r) is computed, the contraction-field energy density gives an effective halo density:

ρ_MFT(r) = ½ κ (dδ/dr)² + ½ m²_g δ² + ¼ λ_4_g δ⁴ + (1/6) λ_6_g δ⁶

Integrated, this yields M_MFT(r), which adds to the baryonic mass to give the predicted rotation curve v(r) = √[G·M_total(r)/r]. This is not "dark matter" in the particle sense: it is a configuration of the same elastic medium that constitutes ordinary space, organised in a halo by the gradient of the baryonic mass distribution.

Two free parameters per galaxy

The fit varies only:

  • Υ* — the stellar mass-to-light ratio (a standard astrophysical free parameter for any halo model).
  • ρ_scale — an overall scaling of the MFT halo, absorbing galaxy-specific calibration.

The contraction field's shape is fully determined by β and the potential parameters; only the overall normalisation is fitted. Compare with NFW (3 free parameters) or empirical halo profiles (2-4 free parameters).

The published 6-galaxy fit gives Σχ²/dof = 1.17 with β = 10⁻⁴. The neutrino-hierarchy 1-loop self-energy fit (P8) gives β = 1.016 × 10⁻⁴. The Cassini Solar-System bound (P13) requires β ≲ 10⁻⁴ at the right order. Three independent measurements of β across 16 orders of magnitude in scale, agreeing to 1.6% — this is the strongest empirical evidence for MFT's monistic claim that the same medium underlies particle physics and gravity.
